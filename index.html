I am a senior student at Colby College studying computer science and astrophysics. My research interest mainly focuses on embodied AI where I explore the application of multi-agent reinforcement learning (MARL) in robotics. I am currently working on my honor thesis with my advisor Ying Li, comparing a cooperative model and a competitive model of MARL for drones' trajectories planning. My interest of embodied AI originates from my passion for art and science. Learning art from a young age gave me a different perspective to interpret and interact the world. Meanwhile, science provides me a logical tool to explore and understand my discoveries. When I found the field of embodied AI, I found a perfect balance to express my creativity and logic.

I come from a small town called Funan in mainland China and I am a first-generation college student. Before entering Colby College, I was fortunate to attend Li Po Chun United World College where I received full scholarship and gained the chance to interact with peers from over 80 different countries. This experience encourages me to think multi-culturally and inclusively which guides my exploration in both academic and extracurricular projects. I hope to incorporate such principles in my future research as well.
