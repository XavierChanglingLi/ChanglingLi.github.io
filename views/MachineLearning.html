My honor thesis explores the application of multi-agent reinforcement learning(MARL) in trajectory planning of drone networks. We modified the Q-learning network to a multi-agent Q learning network with a shared reward across the agents. The goal is to execute the tasks with non-binary length with least energy consumed. The first figure on the left shows the trajectories of 9 agents. The second figure shows the trajectory of a single agent. A more detailed information can be found in the academic poster presented in Colby College Undergraduate Research Retreat in summer of 2021. I am currently exploring the application of MARL when the tasks' locations are random. More of my machine learning related projects can be found in my github.
